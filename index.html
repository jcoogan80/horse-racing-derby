What is Derby Value?

Derby Value analyzes 17,000+ historical exotic wager payouts from 7 racetracks to find sequences where the payout pools have historically overpaid. It tells you which bets have an edge based on real data — not gut feel.

Derby Day: Step by Step
1
Derby Morning — before you leave
Check if today is worth betting

Open the Today's Card tab. Select CD, select P5, select Race 4 as your starting race.

Enter the morning line odds for your top horses in each of races 4, 5, 6, 7, and 8.

Look for the signal at the bottom:
LONGSHOT SETUP The pool is set up for value today.
CHALK ALERT Favorites are expected to dominate — the pool may not pay well. Reduce your bet size or skip.

2
At the betting windows — 30 min before Race 4
Decide how much to spend

Open the Ticket Builder tab. Select CD, P5, starting race 4.

This shows the historical average payout ($64,000), median payout ($15,000), and an overlay rating.

Use the historical median as your realistic target — not the average, which is skewed by rare big hits. Size your ticket accordingly.

3
During the races — if you're alive in your ticket
Estimate what you'll win

If you win races 4, 5, and 6 and are still live: open Ticket Builder, scroll down to Will-Pay Estimator.

Enter the will-pay amount showing on the tote board and the odds of your horses in the remaining legs. It estimates what your ticket will pay if you finish.

Use this to decide whether to hedge before the final race.

4
Final leg — if you're alive going into Race 8
Lock in a guaranteed profit

Open the Hedge Calc tab. Enter your ticket cost, the estimated payout, and the odds of every horse in the field.

It calculates how much to bet on each horse so you profit regardless of who wins.

This is not about breaking even — it's about locking in a guaranteed return on a winning ticket.

The Three Rules
Play P5 at Churchill Downs

The Pick 5 at CD beats a straight parlay 97.6% of race days. The pool generates structural value — bet it every time.

Never play P5 at Keeneland

The parlay beats the KEE Pick 5 pool two out of three days. The pool is too small to generate value — skip it.

Bigger pools = better odds for you

Churchill Downs and Gulfstream Park show exotic-favorable results across all wager types. Smaller tracks do not — stick to the big pools.
