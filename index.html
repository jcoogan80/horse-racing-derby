What is Derby Value?

Derby Value analyzes 18,000+ historical exotic wager payouts from 7 racetracks to find sequences where the payout pools have historically overpaid. It tells you which bets have an edge based on real data — not gut feel.

Derby Day: Step by Step
1
Derby Morning — before you leave
Check if today is worth betting

Open the Today's Card tab. Select CD, select P5, select Race 4 as your starting race.

Enter the morning line odds for your top horses in each of races 4, 5, 6, 7, and 8.

Look for the signal at the bottom:
LONGSHOT SETUP The pool is set up for value today.
CHALK ALERT Favorites are expected to dominate — the pool may not pay well. Reduce your bet size or skip.

2
Night before or Derby morning — before you go to the windows
Build Your Ticket

This is the most important step. Before you buy anything:

Go to the Today's Card tab. Select CD, Pick 5, starting race 4. Morning lines auto-populate for each leg.

Type different ticket combinations in the format:
4,6 / 1,3 / 2 / 7,8 / 3

The tool scores each ticket instantly:
LONGSHOT SETUP The combination is priced above the historical average — overlay potential.
CHALK ALERT Favorites dominate this combination — the pool likely won't pay well.

Test 4–5 different combinations in under 2 minutes. Buy the ticket that scores LONGSHOT SETUP at a cost you're comfortable with.

The goal is to structure your ticket using the data before you go to the window — not to analyze tickets you already bought. A $16 ticket with a LONGSHOT SETUP signal is better than a $48 ticket with a CHALK ALERT.

★

Build before you buy — the tool is most powerful when used before the race, not after.

3
During the races — if you're alive in your ticket
Estimate what you'll win

If you win races 4, 5, and 6 and are still live: open Ticket Builder, scroll down to Will-Pay Estimator.

Enter the will-pay amount showing on the tote board and the odds of your horses in the remaining legs. It estimates what your ticket will pay if you finish.

Use this to decide whether to hedge before the final race.

4
Final leg — if you're alive going into Race 8
Lock in a guaranteed profit

Open the Hedge Calc tab. Enter your ticket cost, the estimated payout, and the odds of every horse in the field.

It calculates how much to bet on each horse so you profit regardless of who wins.

This is not about breaking even — it's about locking in a guaranteed return on a winning ticket.

The Three Rules
Play P5 at Churchill Downs

The Pick 5 at CD beats a straight parlay 97.6% of race days. The pool generates structural value — bet it every time.

Never play P5 at Keeneland

The parlay beats the KEE Pick 5 pool two out of three days. The pool is too small to generate value — skip it.

Bigger pools = better odds for you

Churchill Downs and Gulfstream Park show exotic-favorable results across all wager types. Smaller tracks do not — stick to the big pools.

AE Horse Alerts
What is an AE alert?

When a horse is scratched from a race, the next horse on the also-eligible list draws in. That horse gets a program number higher than the original field size — a 13 in a 12-horse race, or an 11 in a 10-horse race.

Why it matters

The betting public rarely reprices properly after a late scratch. AE horses are systematically underbet. When they do win, they pay 2–6x the field average. At Churchill Downs, the one AE winner in a 12-horse turf field paid $71 against a $12 field average.

How to use the alert

When you see a yellow AE alert banner on the Today's Card tab, check the odds on that horse at the window. If it's going off at 15-1 or higher on a turf race, it's worth a small win bet or including as a longshot single in one leg of your exotic. Below 15-1, the math doesn't support it.
